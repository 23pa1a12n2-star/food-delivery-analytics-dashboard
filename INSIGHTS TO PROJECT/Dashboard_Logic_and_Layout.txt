===============================================================
DASHBOARD LOGIC & LAYOUT GUIDE — FOOD DELIVERY ANALYTICS
===============================================================

1. KPI CALCULATIONS & LOGIC
---------------------------
- Total Revenue: 
  Logic: Sum of (Order Value + Delivery Fee) for all orders.
  Formula: SUM([Order Value]) + SUM([Delivery Fee])

- Average Order Value (AOV): 
  Logic: The average base value of an order (excluding delivery fee).
  Formula: AVG([Order Value])

- Order Volume by City: 
  Logic: The total count of unique Order IDs grouped by the City dimension.
  Formula: COUNT([Order ID]) grouped by [City]

- Average Delivery Time by City: 
  Logic: The mean duration for delivery execution grouped by City.
  Formula: AVG([Time Taken for Delivery]) grouped by [City]

- Customer Rating Derivation: 
  Logic: Since organic ratings were unavailable, ratings were inverse-mapped to delivery efficiency as a proxy for satisfaction.
  Formula: IF [Time] < 20 THEN 5, ELIF <= 30 THEN 4, ELIF <= 40 THEN 3, ELIF <= 50 THEN 2, ELSE 1.

2. VISUALIZATION STRATEGY
---------------------------
Evaluating the design selection for specific charts:
- KPI Dashboard (Text Summary): Best for immediate high-level "glance value." Stakeholders need top-line numbers boldly displayed before analyzing granular trends.
- Flavors/Cities & City Demand (Bar Charts): Vertical and grouped bar charts effectively display magnitude differences across discrete nominal categories without cognitive overload.
- Order Distribution (Stacked/Segmented Countplots): Shows cross-sections of demographics (Age) vs. buying power (Segment) seamlessly.
- Cuisine Preferences (Pie Chart/Donut): Highly effective here specifically because the share is evenly split (~16.6% each), perfectly illustrating symmetric market distribution.
- Customer Satisfaction (Boxplots): Superior for showing the median rating, distribution spread, and outliers across Meal Types compared to a simple bar chart.
- Delivery Performance (Histogram/KDE): Critical for revealing the 40-60 minute bottleneck. A continuous density plot perfectly highlights volume clusters that averages would hide.

3. RECOMMENDED DASHBOARD WIREFRAME (Tableau / BI Tool)
------------------------------------------------------
To maximize stakeholder impact, the final interactive dashboard should follow a strict F-pattern visual hierarchy:

# TOP SECTION: Global Controls & Executive Summary (The "What")
- [Top-Left]: Global Filters (Date Range, City, Company Type, Meal Type).
- [Top-Center to Right]: Large KPI Banner Cards: 
  [ TOTAL REVENUE ]  |  [ TOTAL ORDERS ]  |  [ AOV ]  |  [ AVG DELIVERY TIME ]

# MIDDLE SECTION: Revenue & Operations Analysis (The "Where & When")
- [Left Column (Wide)]: Revenue Contribution by Meal Type & Flavors Across Cities. (Focus for Rahul)
- [Right Column (Narrow)]: Operational Analysis (Company Type vs. Meal Type). (Focus for Priya)

# BOTTOM SECTION: Experience & Performance Diagnosis (The "Why")
- [Left Panel]: Delivery Performance Distribution Histogram. (The Core Bottleneck)
- [Right Panel]: Customer Satisfaction Boxplots (Ratings by Meal Type).
  > Visual linkage: Directly placing delivery times next to customer ratings makes the correlation between slow dispatch and 2.6-star reviews painfully obvious to decision-makers.
